    Music 27 Quiz 2 Revision

    A Form refers to one of many standardized patterns that composers use.

    Fugue

    A Fugue is a polyphonic composition for a fixed number of instrumental lines or voices on a single principal theme, which is called the fugue subject.

    Diagram: A A A

    Fugue Exposition: A fugue begins with an exposition in which all the voices present the subject in an orderly, standardized way.

    Prelude: Each fugue is preceded by an introductory piece, or prelude, in the same key and mode.

    Ritornello

    Many Baroqe concerto mevements, especially the first and the third, are in ritornello form, which discusses the contrast among some musical ideas.

    Diagram: T S dT S dT where T = tutti, 'all', which is the entire ritornello, dT is part of the ritornello, and solo

    concerto grosso: instrumental work staging contrast between orchestral ensemble and smaller group or one solo instrument.

    Movement of Baroque Concerto: * Opening: Ritornello * Slow Movement: No standard form * Closing Movement: Ritornello

    Cadenza is an improvised passage within a larger piece, a feature of concertos.

    Da Capo Aria

    An Aria is a set piece for solo singers. Opera Seria is the mian type of Baroque opera, meaning serious opera.

    The standard form of Baroque Italian opera aria is da capo aira. Da capo means repeat from the beginning. Diagram: A B A

    Castrato: men submitted castration at puberty to preserve their voices in the soprano/alto range.

    Sonata

    The second part of the eighteenth century, the Classical style emerged.

    A symphony is a classical period style of a large impressive concert piece for orchestra.

    Movement of Symphony: * Opening: Fast, sonata * Slow Movement: slow, Sonata/rondo/other variation * Minuet(with Trio): Moderate, minuet form * Closing Movement: Fast, sonate/rondo

    Sonata form: is a very large-scale example of the A B A' form.

    Exposition (A): present the basic material of the movement. Firstly, the main theme is presented in the first key (called the first theme), then a modulation happens, which is called a bridge or a translation. Then another musical idea is introduced as the second group. The second group has a cadence/closing theme, for a solid ending.

    Development (B): develop the themes in exposition by playing around with them. Typically use counterpoint to create a sense of breakup. The last modulation retransitions into the first key.

    Recapitulation (A'): the first theme back again, followed by other exposition themes with minor changes.

    Coda: a concluding section for wrapping up.

    Rondo

    Mainly used for fast closing movements. A rondo begins witha ful0fledged tune (A) and regualarly returns to it after each episode.

    Diagram: A B A C A conda

    Minuet: A stately dance in triple meter, with grace and ease. A Baroque minuet consit of two section, |a||b|, and come in pairs with trio, a special minuet as in early days it was often played by 3 instruments. Diagram: A B A |a||b| |c||d| |a||b| Here, B is called trio.
